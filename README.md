## Malaria Prevalence Mapping Using GIS – Kogi State, Nigeria

## Overview
This project analyzes the spatial distribution of malaria prevalence in Kogi West, Nigeria using Geographic Information Systems (GIS).

Malaria remains a major public health challenge, especially in sub-Saharan Africa, where environmental and socio-economic factors strongly influence disease spread :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0}.

## Objectives
- Identify factors influencing malaria prevalence  
- Analyze spatial patterns of malaria distribution  
- Map vulnerable areas using GIS  

## Data & Methodology
- Survey data from 400 respondents across 7 LGAs  
- GIS analysis using ArcGIS 10.8  
- Spatial modelling using:
  - Waste disposal patterns  
  - Drainage conditions  
  - Land use/land cover  

- Risk model applied:
  R = H × E × V  

## Tools Used
- ArcGIS 10.8  
- GIS Spatial Analysis  
- Descriptive Statistics  

## Key Findings
- ~60% of the study area is at high malaria risk  
- Lokoja and Kogi LGAs are most vulnerable  
- Major drivers include:
  - Poor drainage  
  - Improper waste disposal  
  - Low income levels  

## Outputs
- Malaria vulnerability maps  
- Spatial risk classification (Low → Very High)  
- Policy-relevant insights
